> [!Intersection of Events]
>- The event $A\cap{B}$ is the **intersection** of $A$ and $B$
>- That mean it contain the outcomes from $A$ and $B$ at same time.
>- In conclusion, $P(A\cap{B})$ is probability that both event $A$ and $B$ **happen at same time**
>- If $A\cap{B} = \emptyset$, it's mean event $A$ and $B$ is **mutually exclusive**

> [!Additional Formula]
> $$
> P(A'\cap{B}) + P(A\cap{B}) = P(B)
> $$
> $$
> P(A\cap{B'}) + P(A\cap{B}) = P(A)
> $$
> $$
> A\cap{(B\cap{C})} = A\cap{B\cap{C}} = (A\cap{B})\cap{C}
> $$
> - The formula got from [[Conditional Probability]] definition:
> $$
> P(A\cap{B}) = P(B){P(A|B)}
> $$
