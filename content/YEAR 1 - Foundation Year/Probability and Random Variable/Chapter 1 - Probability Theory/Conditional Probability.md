> [!Formula]
>- The probability of event **A** while even **B** is *occurring* is:
> $$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A\cap{B})}{P(B)}$$
> 
>- With $P(B)>0$



> [!Properties of Conditional Probability]
> $P(A|B) + P(A'|B) = 1$

## Easier explanation

- Let's imagine that if event $B$ is **occurring**, that's mean the sample space had reduce from $S=A\cup{B}$ to $S=B$
- Let event $C$ be the [[Intersections of Events]] $A$ and $B$ => $P(C) = P(A\cap{B})$

> [!Formula after explain]
> $$P(A) = \frac{P(C)}{S}$$
> With:
> - $P(C) = P(A\cap{B})$
> - $S = P(B)$
> - But still maintain $S > 0$
